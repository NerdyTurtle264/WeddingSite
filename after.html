Post Ceremony

After the ceremony, many portraits were taken. The full extent of them isn’t available yet, but here are some of them.


First Dance

The first dance was eagerly anticipated and Alison has had the song picked out for years. It was only slightly interrupted when not twenty seconds into it, the bustle of the dress broke! This left the couple to creatively dance around the train.


Father Daughter Dance

One more dance marked the day, that of Alison and her father Michael. When she was a child, Michael would try to soothe her to sleep with music, however to anyone who knows him, he is not musically inclined. Not knowing any nursery songs, Michael sang what he knew: classic rock. Eventually the only song he sang to her was a heavily modified version of Sailing Away by Styx. Here they dance to the part of the song used as her childhood lullaby. The two stop about halfway through the song as that was where Michael always cut it off, unable to sing a guitar solo.


The License

Due to some quirks of the Pennsylvania legal system, the bride and groom were not wed by the power of a religious figure, but by their own will. Using a self-sealing marriage license designed for the Quakers, Alison and Johnny pledged themselves in marriage and were witnessed by two loving friends who signed as well, pledging the truth of their commitment.


The Cake

Confectionaries for the day were twofold: The wedding cake was made by Megan McIntosh, the bride’s cousin, and the vegan cupcakes were from Half Dozen Bakery halfdozencafe.com Sitting atop the cake are two one-inch scale model miniatures that the couple designed of themselves and had 3D printed.


Toasts

Michael made a heartfelt toast welcoming in not just Johnny, who he has long called his son, but the new machatonim, Johnny’s family. After that, Johnny’s father Dave came over for a private toast, calling Alison his daughter and welcoming her to the family.


Dinner

Food is the highlight of many a Lashinsky gathering and this one was no different. An assortment of mostly vegan food was provided by Russos Market.


Following dinner, the photographer and some guests left. Those who remained got comfortable: showers and naps were had all around and by six that night, the family reconvened for a quiet evening of doing puzzles and watching a fire burn.

The morning after the ceremony was bittersweet. The whole day felt like such a blur as everyone packed for their journey home.
